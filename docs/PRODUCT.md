# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fragrance enthusiasts, gift-seekers, and curious buyers who are done with department-store sameness. They are drawn to provenance, process, and singular objects. They read ingredient lists, follow independent makers, and spend time choosing — not converting. When they open Vency Atelier, they are not shopping; they are discovering.

## Product Purpose

Vency Atelier is a Costa Rican fragrance house dedicated to the art of slow perfumery. Each composition is hand-blended in small batches using raw materials sourced from independent suppliers worldwide. The studio creates both inspired compositions and original narratives — olfactory stories designed to linger, not to trend. The site exists to communicate that identity, earn trust, and invite people into the world of the atelier before they ever uncap a bottle.

## Brand Personality

Bold, deliberate, alive. The atelier is proud of its origins and its methods — not in a loud way, but in the way of someone who knows exactly what they are doing and does not need to explain it twice. The voice is confident without being cold, artisan without being rustic, Costa Rican without being folkloric.

## Anti-references

- **Department-store perfumery**: bright whites, celebrity campaigns, glass-counter sterility, generic lifestyle photography. No blank clinical surfaces.
- **Maximalist luxury (Versace, D&G)**: baroque ornamentation, heavy gold, logomania. Vency's craft is the statement — not its decoration.
- **Startup / SaaS aesthetic**: rounded sans-serifs, gradient CTAs, "Join 10,000 customers" social proof, conversion-rate copywriting. This is an atelier, not a funnel.
- **Generic e-commerce (Shopify defaults)**: breadcrumb trails, product-grid orthodoxy, stock lifestyle photography, reviews-as-first-impression. The experience should feel like visiting a studio, not a storefront.

## Design Principles

1. **The making is the message.** Process, materials, and the hands behind each bottle should be legible in every design choice — not as decoration, but as structure.
2. **Slow is a stance.** No urgency, no countdown timers, no conversion pressure. The design should breathe at the same pace as a fragrance drying down on skin.
3. **Provenance over polish.** The independence and specificity of a Costa Rican atelier is a feature, not an apology. Roughness and distinctiveness are assets; smooth sameness is the competitor's problem.
4. **Every fragrance is a story first.** Narrative leads; product follows. No fragrance should appear without its world.
5. **Confident restraint.** Bold choices, sparsely deployed. One strong thing per screen is more powerful than five polished things.
