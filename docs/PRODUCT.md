# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Fragrance enthusiasts, gift-seekers, and curious buyers who are done with department-store sameness. They are drawn to provenance, process, and singular objects. They read ingredient lists, follow independent makers, and spend time choosing — not converting. When they open Vency Atelier, they are not shopping; they are discovering.

## Product Purpose

Vency Atelier is a Costa Rican fragrance house dedicated to the art of slow perfumery. Each composition is hand-blended in small batches using raw materials sourced from independent suppliers worldwide. The studio creates both inspired compositions and original narratives — olfactory stories designed to linger, not to trend.

The model is a **laboratorio**, not a workshop. The fragrances are pre-mixed at the atelier in Heredia and ready to ship — there is no bespoke chemistry session, no in-store blending wizard, no name-on-the-bottle service. What makes each bottle personal is that it was composed by a known hand, in a small batch, in a known place. Specificity of provenance does the work of bespoke.

The site exists to communicate that identity, earn trust, and invite people into the world of the atelier before they ever uncap a bottle.

## Brand Personality

Bold, deliberate, alive. The atelier is proud of its origins and its methods — not in a loud way, but in the way of someone who knows exactly what they are doing and does not need to explain it twice. The voice is confident without being cold, artisan without being rustic, Costa Rican without being folkloric.

Think of a laboratory bench more than a salon — Le Labo's atelier, not a perfumer's studio session. The fragrance is composed; the customer chooses. Nothing on the site or in the experience should promise a personal blend, a guided composition, or a one-on-one workshop. The intimacy comes from the small-batch reality of how the bottle in front of you was made — not from a service the customer participates in.

## Anti-references

- **Department-store perfumery**: bright whites, celebrity campaigns, glass-counter sterility, generic lifestyle photography. No blank clinical surfaces.
- **Maximalist luxury (Versace, D&G)**: baroque ornamentation, heavy gold, logomania. Vency's craft is the statement — not its decoration.
- **Startup / SaaS aesthetic**: rounded sans-serifs, gradient CTAs, "Join 10,000 customers" social proof, conversion-rate copywriting. This is an atelier, not a funnel.
- **Generic e-commerce (Shopify defaults)**: breadcrumb trails, product-grid orthodoxy, stock lifestyle photography, reviews-as-first-impression. The experience should feel like visiting a studio, not a storefront.
- **Bespoke / custom-blending services**: name-on-the-label personalization wizards, "design your fragrance" multi-step quizzes, dual-screen in-store consultations, perfumer-led one-on-one sessions, or affiliate-influencer "your personal scent" upsells. Vency's promise is the opposite — a finished composition, made in a small batch, in Heredia, ready to ship. The label says the name of the fragrance and the name of the atelier. Nothing more.

## Design Principles

1. **The making is the message.** Process, materials, and the hands behind each bottle should be legible in every design choice — not as decoration, but as structure.
2. **Slow is a stance.** No urgency, no countdown timers, no conversion pressure. The design should breathe at the same pace as a fragrance drying down on skin.
3. **Provenance over polish, batch over bespoke.** The independence and specificity of a Costa Rican atelier is a feature, not an apology. Roughness and distinctiveness are assets; smooth sameness is the competitor's problem. The "personal" in this brand is structural, not a service: every bottle is small-batch, hand-blended, hand-labelled at the atelier in Heredia. That is the entire intimacy story. No customization wizard, no Sesión de Atelier, no bespoke composition — those would dilute the proposition by promising something the laboratorio model deliberately does not offer.
4. **Every fragrance is a story first.** Narrative leads; product follows. No fragrance should appear without its world.
5. **Confident restraint.** Bold choices, sparsely deployed. One strong thing per screen is more powerful than five polished things.
